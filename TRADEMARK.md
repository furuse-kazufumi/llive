and their stylized logos, when present — are **trademarks of Kazufumi Furuse**
(the "Owner").

This document explains what you may and may not do with these marks. It
does not grant or restrict any rights to the *software* itself; those are
governed by `LICENSE` (Apache-2.0) and `LICENSE-COMMERCIAL`.

## Brand hierarchy

```
                  FullSense ™   (umbrella brand & spec name)
                  /     |     \
              llmesh   llive   llove   ™ ™ ™   (product marks)
                |        |        |
            secure    self-     TUI
            LLM hub   evolving  dashboard
                      memory
                      LLM
```

- **FullSense** is the parent brand and the name of the underlying
  technical specification ("FullSense Spec v1.1", `docs/fullsense_spec_eternal.md`).
- **llmesh / llive / llove** are products that implement, extend, or
  consume FullSense Spec.

This dual-use (brand + spec name) is intentional and is recommended
practice for showing that the brand carries technical substance —
analogous to how "Linux" names both the kernel and the foundation that
stewards it.

## What you may do without asking

- Refer to **FullSense**, llmesh, llive, or llove by name in articles, blog
  posts, presentations, papers, social media, and similar nominative use.
- Use the names to truthfully describe that your project, service, or
  tutorial is *about* or *built on top of* FullSense or any of its
  products ("compatible with FullSense", "powered by llive", "for use
  with llmesh").
- Distribute unmodified copies of any of these projects under the
  Apache-2.0 license, retaining the original marks in `NOTICE` and source
  files.
- Refer to "FullSense Spec" or "the FullSense family" as collective
  descriptors.

## What requires permission

- Using "FullSense", "llmesh", "llive", or "llove" as the **name** of your
  fork, derivative, hosted service, or product. Forks should rename
  themselves (e.g. `<yourname>-llive`, `llive-community-edition`).
- Using the marks in a way that implies official endorsement, sponsorship,
  certification, or partnership.
- Registering domain names, social-media handles, or trademark
  registrations containing the marks in classes that overlap with the
  Owner's planned use (software, SaaS, AI/LLM tooling — primarily Nice
  classes 9 and 42).
- Modifying or stylizing the marks in promotional materials.

To request permission, contact `kazufumi@furuse.work` with a description
of the intended use and the duration.

## Why a trademark policy?

A liberal open-source license (Apache-2.0) lets anyone use, modify, and
redistribute the code. A trademark policy lets the community trust that
something named "FullSense" or "llive" actually behaves like the
original project — i.e. is the official release or a permitted variant.
The two work together: **code is free, the name signals provenance**.

This policy is modelled on policies of similar OSS projects (Mozilla,
Python Software Foundation, Apache Software Foundation, Rust Foundation)
and reflects long-standing OSS community norms.
